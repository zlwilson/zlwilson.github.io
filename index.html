designer, artist, programmer; computer science, human-computer interaction design, fine art.

I'm currently pursuing a Masters of Science in Human-Computer Interaction Design at the University of Twente in the Netherlands.

For my Bachelor’s degree I began studying computer science.

I’ve always enjoyed mathematics and building things, so I fit right in there.

Soon after starting however I discovered the art program at my university. I began with a couple simple courses, but then I met a professor whose practice centered on how humanity was changing, or had changed through its relationship and interactions with technology.

This quickly became the focus of my undergraduate degree, though practical fine art as well as studies in the history of art and technology.

I always kept up my computer science education however as it seemed crucial to be able to practice and understand computers from the inside out, even as I studied it from the outside in.

All this is to say that my Bachelor’s degree evolved into a five year research study into the relationship between humans and computers, both from the development of applications and research in a multi-modal user interaction lab, as well as through studies in history and my own artistic practice.
